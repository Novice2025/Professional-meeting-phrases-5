Scenario 1: The Project Kick-off

You are starting a new project. You need to tell the team that you will be the main person in charge, that the project needs to be broken down into smaller tasks, and that you expect the team to start working immediately.

  • To be the project lead: "I'll **run point** on this project and manage the next steps."
  • To break down the project: "First, we need to **granularize** the main objectives into a detailed plan."
  • To start working efficiently: "I know you're all prepared, so let's **hit the ground running** right away."

Scenario 2: The Strategic Planning Session

You are in a meeting discussing a new business model. You want to suggest that a new, unconventional approach is needed, but you want to avoid a huge, complex project. You also need to confirm that you have everyone's approval for the proposal.

  • To define the scope of the new approach: "Before we proceed, we need to **scope out a project** to define our new approach."
  • To avoid a complex project: "We should be careful not to **boil the ocean**; let's start with a smaller, more focused project."
  • To close the loop with approval: "Once we have the final numbers, we can present this to the board to **close the loop** on our proposal."

Scenario 3: The Project Review

You are discussing a project's performance. You need to explain that a recent action had a significant impact on sales and that you need to investigate the details to understand why. You also need to remind a colleague to close the loop with the client.

  • To discuss impact: "The new campaign really seemed to **move the needle** on our sales."
  • To investigate details: "The data looks promising, but we need to **drill down** into the specifics to understand the cause."
  • To close a conversation: "Don't forget to **take this offline** with the client about the new deliverables."
